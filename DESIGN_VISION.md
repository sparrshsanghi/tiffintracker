# Design Vision

## North Star

Maa Sharda should feel like opening a carefully packed home lunch that was prepared for you by someone who knows your routine.

This is not a CRUD dashboard. It is a daily ritual, a membership, and a quiet relationship with food.

## Product Feeling

The product should immediately communicate:

- My lunch is taken care of.
- I do not need to manage this every day.
- I trust what is happening.
- The experience is personal, calm, and premium.

## Design Thesis

The visual system must balance three things at once:

1. Warmth that feels human and homemade.
2. Precision that feels trustworthy and organized.
3. Restraint that keeps the product premium instead of busy.

The best version of Maa Sharda should feel crafted, not designed in a generic product template.

## Emotional Arc

Each morning the user should move through a small emotional sequence:

1. Anticipation.
2. Recognition.
3. Relief.
4. Confidence.
5. Light delight.

That arc should appear first on Today, then repeat in smaller ways across AI, History, and Profile.

## Visual Priorities

The visual system should prioritize:

- The tiffin as the hero object.
- Food as the proof of care.
- Soft motion as a daily ritual.
- Typography that feels editorial, not app-like.
- Surfaces that feel tactile and composed.

## What It Must Avoid

The design should never feel like:

- A fintech app.
- A SaaS admin panel.
- A logistics dashboard.
- A chat support tool.
- A generic mobile subscription app.

## Signature Moment

The opening tiffin sequence is the brand signature.

It should feel like a small ceremony:

Closed tiffin.
Opening reveal.
Steam and warmth.
Meal revealed.
Delivery context.

That moment is the emotional proof of the brand.

## Experience Principles

- Make the meal the first emotional object on screen.
- Let status live around the food, not above it.
- Use calm spacing and soft contrast to reduce stress.
- Treat the customer as a known household, not a ticket number.
- Keep manager surfaces crisp and operational, but never cold or punitive.

## Recommended Direction

Direction B, Modern Indian, should be the primary system.

It best carries the name Maa Sharda, the warmth of home-style food, and the premium confidence the product needs.

Direction A can inspire material restraint and product photography discipline.

Direction C can inspire the final level of simplicity.

The final product should blend all three influences, but the identity should lean Indian first.

## Success Test

If a person sees one screen and says, "This feels like lunch at home, but elevated," the vision is working.

If they instead say, "This looks like an app for managing orders," the vision has failed.
