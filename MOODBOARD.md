# Moodboard

## Mood Keywords

Warm
Calm
Home-cooked
Premium
Assured
Tactile
Sunlit
Human
Quietly joyful

## Material References

- Cream paper
- Brass accents
- Ceramic lunch containers
- Soft wood grain
- Brushed steel
- Woven cloth textures
- Matte glass

## Food References

- Steaming rice
- Dal with a soft sheen
- Sabzi with texture and color depth
- Roti stacked with care
- Lunch plates that feel freshly served
- Condensation on a warm lid

## Photography Direction

- Use close crops and intimate framing.
- Prefer natural light over studio glare.
- Keep shadows soft and believable.
- Capture the tiffin as an object with presence, not as a generic prop.
- Avoid over-styled restaurant hero shots.

## Illustration Direction

- Small, precise, food-tied illustrations.
- Steam, trays, lids, route marks, and approval stamps are the strongest motifs.
- Avoid decorative illustration that does not connect to the service.

## Atmosphere References

- A clean kitchen just after lunch is packed.
- A quiet morning before delivery begins.
- A warm corridor or threshold where food is handed over.
- A neatly organized household routine.

## Emotional References

- Relief when lunch is already handled.
- Comfort when the routine is familiar.
- Confidence when status is clear.
- Delight when the tiffin opens.
- Security when requests are remembered.

## Brand Objects

- Closed tiffin stack
- Open tiffin compartments
- Steam
- Delivery route line
- Approval stamp
- Meal note card
- Memory thread

## Do Not Use

- Generic office dashboards
- Neon fintech colors
- Overly polished stock food imagery
- Loud cartoon mascots
- Abstract blobs that have no service meaning
- Heavy glassmorphism

## Moodboard Summary

The ideal board should feel like a premium Indian lunch ritual translated into a modern, mobile-first product language.

It should not look aspirational in a luxury fashion sense.

It should look cared for, intimate, and dependable.
