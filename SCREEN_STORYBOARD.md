# Screen Storyboard

## Storyboard Intent

This is a narrative blueprint for how the product should feel as the user moves through it.

It is not an implementation plan.

## Today

### Opening Frame

- The user enters to a calm greeting and a closed tiffin.
- The screen should feel like the start of lunch, not like a dashboard.

### Ritual Beat

- The tiffin opens.
- Steam appears.
- The meal is revealed in a beautiful, composed way.
- This is the emotional center of the product.

### Context Beat

- Delivery information appears after the reveal.
- The meal should still remain the visual anchor.

### Action Beat

- Quick actions appear as helpful next steps.
- AI is introduced as a support layer, not the main event.

### Feeling

- Relief.
- Familiarity.
- Confidence.

## Onboarding

### Opening Frame

- The experience begins like a conversation with Maa.
- No form grid, no checklist energy, no account setup language.

### Conversation Beat

- The system asks one kind, simple question at a time.
- Responses should feel like a natural exchange.

### Profile Formation Beat

- As the user answers, the profile assembles itself quietly in the background.
- The product should appear to understand routine as it goes.

### Completion Beat

- The user should finish onboarding with the sense that the service already knows how to care for them.

## AI

### Opening Frame

- AI feels like someone who remembers the household pattern.

### Conversation Beat

- The user writes naturally.
- The product responds with understanding, not synthetic charm.

### Confirmation Beat

- When a request is extracted, the UI should make the customer feel safely guided.

### Feeling

- Understood.
- Respected.
- Not interrogated.

## Profile

### Opening Frame

- Profile should feel like membership, not settings.

### Information Beat

- Show stable identity, preferences, and service details as belonging to the household relationship.

### Feeling

- Established.
- Trusted.
- Permanent enough to feel reassuring.

## History

### Opening Frame

- History should feel like looking back at moments the service kept for you.

### Memory Beat

- Each entry should feel meaningful and safe to revisit.
- The design should avoid logbook energy.

### Feeling

- Remembered.
- Looked after.

## Manager Philosophy

### Opening Frame

- The manager product should feel like a disciplined work console.

### Operational Beat

- Dense information is allowed when it improves speed and clarity.
- The interface should never borrow the customer mood if it slows decisions.

### Emotional Goal

- Calm authority.
- Fast comprehension.
- Clear next action.

## Storyboard Summary

The customer journey should feel like daily care.

The manager journey should feel like operational control.

Both must belong to the same brand, but they should not look or behave the same.
